About Me

I am a new transfer student who started this semester and I plan on pursuing a Computer Science major. Something you should know about me is that I enjoy learning new things and adding them to my toolbelt. This Composition II class lets students get a bit creative with their writing and I want to make the most of that, as it can help connect the themes of writing with other subjects that interest me. Even though reading and writing isn't my forte, I see it as something to challenge myself and improve upon.

If I had to describe my journey when it comes to learning and building skills that can be applied outside of a writing class, it involves a lot of self-discovery and learning experiences about subject matter as well as my own personal writing techniques. I plan to illustrate this theme of discovery by illustrating an experience similar to my own and showing my discoveries in a way that is reflective of my own experience and growth as a writer.

Foundation for Success

Just like the activities prior that helped me to prepare for bigger assignments, these two assignments played a big role in laying down the foundation and doing some preemptive work that would make the next corresponding assignment more manageable. What I discovered about myself as a result of these kind of assignments is how much I succumb to assignments that appear daunting. I realize now how effective splitting up work can be for my productivity and mentality, but trying to split a big assignment up on my own usually backfires since you almost have to hardwire your brain into thinking it's not one big assignment with a set deadline, but rather 2 smaller assignments with deadlines that you decided on. Because of this inability to mentally split up work, I am unable to take advantage of this workflow. After reflecting upon the reason why I am unable to split up my work, I believe it falls upon my procrastination and perfectionist tendencies which have me submitting assignments very close to their appointed deadlines and if I try to establish my own deadlines, I end up going past those deadlines because "make believe" deadlines don't hold as much weight in my conscious and it becomes too easy to brush off deadlines if they don't have much weight behind them.


To summarize, creating a foundational assignment which can then be built upon by a proceeding assignment is an effective way of splitting up a large assignment into more manageable pieces. With my tendencies, I struggle to split up assignments on my own, so I appreciate when teachers design the course to help us in that way.



Synthesis Chart


The Synthesis Chart was an assignment that tested my ability to find and evaluate scholarly sources that were credible and relevant to my research paper, which falls in line with the course outcome of [3] Information Literacy. This assignment and the one that follows it, Literature Review & Annotated Bibliography (Feedback draft), were the most challenging for me likely because of the fact that it involved finding scholarly articles that were relevant to my area of interest was not easy in the slightest. The main issues that arose were that not many articles have been published on my area of interest and the sources that I did end up finding could be considered a stretch since they didn't directly correlate with my subject area but they were similar enough to the subject matter. I discovered from this assignment that not everything is going to go your way and that sometimes you need to get creative and do what makes you uncomfortable in order to push through undecisive moments.

Roadblocks

Whenever dealing with new challenges, you are bound to run into roadblocks. That is exactly what happened to me when it came to finding the right scholarly sources and making sure they fit with our understanding of [3] Information Literacy. I started discovering more about the way I work and my tendencies to read slow, write slow and to overall be needlessly critical when it came to evaluating things. When it comes to dealing with these roadblocks, I've found that it is best to reflect on why you are experiencing them and fixing the source of your problem. In my case, it was an issue of being too critical, and not feeling enough urgency which resulted in my work flow being rather slow and my priorities were set on doing a good job by my standards rather than meeting a deadline.
